SLAP Art Collection

Artist Statement

SLAP is an ongoing series of hand-made collages built around a single recurring element: the Magical Flag of Peace. Each piece is assembled by hand — stickers placed deliberately on paper, cardstock, or other substrates — and no two are identical.

The project began as an exploration of repetition and variation: what happens when the same image is placed in slightly different configurations, on different surfaces, with different numbers of copies? What emerges from constraint? The flag is fixed. Everything else is open.

Over time the collection has grown into something closer to a body of research — a catalog of combinations, a meditation on symmetry and chance, and a record of moments when a piece was given away and went on to live somewhere else.

Tag Guide

Surface
Size Physical dimensions of the substrate, width × height in inches.
Orientation Whether the substrate is wider than tall (horizontal), taller than wide (vertical), or equal (square).
Material The physical material the stickers are applied to — paper, cardstock, canvas board, etc.
Color Background color of the substrate.
Border Whether the substrate has a pre-printed decorative border.
Laminate Whether the finished piece has been laminated for protection.
Design
Stickers The number of flag stickers used in the piece.
Symmetry The 2D point group symmetry of the overall arrangement. 360° means rotationally symmetric at any angle. 180° looks the same when rotated half a turn. 1 Mirror and 2 Mirrors indicate reflective symmetry across one or two axes.
Pattern The compositional arrangement of the stickers — stack, offset, linear, unit, or none.
Pattern Orientation How the pattern is oriented relative to the substrate surface — horizontal, vertical, or diagonal.
Flag Orientation The direction the flag faces within each sticker — aligned (as printed), flipped (mirrored), or self (orientation inherent to the sticker itself).
Spin A measure of total sticker orientation — the sum of rotation angles applied across all stickers in the piece. Positive values are clockwise, negative counter-clockwise, 0 is upright. Undefined means spin was not recorded.
Shape The overall silhouette formed by the arrangement of stickers — convex, concave, or infinite.
Flag Version Which print run of the flag sticker was used. V1, V2, and V3 have subtle design differences.
Signature The artist's signature style used on this piece.
Owner
Owner The person this piece was given or sold to, if it has left the artist's collection.
Date The date the piece was transferred to its owner.
Price Sale price, if applicable. Gifted pieces have no price recorded.
Note Any additional context about the transfer.

Rarity Index

Each SLAP is assigned a Rarity Index — a score reflecting how statistically unusual its combination of design attributes is across the entire collection.

The score is calculated by dividing the total SLAP count by the number of pieces sharing each tag value, then summing those quotients across all design attributes. Attributes with fewer matching pieces contribute a larger amount to the score — rarer combinations produce higher totals.

Rarity Index = Σ ( total SLAPs ÷ pieces sharing that value ) for each design attribute

The Percentile expresses where a piece falls relative to the full collection — a piece at the 99th percentile has a rarer combination of attributes than 99% of all SLAPs. The Rank is its ordinal position when sorted by rarity index.

As the collection grows, scores automatically recalibrate — shifting individual rankings and percentiles accordingly.
